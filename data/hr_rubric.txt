===========================
HR INTERVIEW RUBRIC & GUIDELINES
===========================

SECTION 1: GENERAL INTERVIEW PRINCIPLES
-----------------------------------------
- Interviews should assess candidates fairly based on competency, not subjective bias.
- Use behavioral questions (STAR method: Situation, Task, Action, Result).
- Evaluate both technical fit and cultural alignment.
- All candidates for the same role should be asked the same core questions.
- Interviewers must remain neutral and avoid leading questions.

SECTION 2: CORE HR COMPETENCIES TO ASSESS
-------------------------------------------
1. Communication Skills
   - Can the candidate articulate ideas clearly?
   - Do they listen actively and respond thoughtfully?
   - Scoring: 1 (Poor) to 5 (Excellent)

2. Problem-Solving Ability
   - Can they break down complex problems logically?
   - Do they demonstrate creative thinking?
   - Scoring: 1 (Poor) to 5 (Excellent)

3. Teamwork & Collaboration
   - Have they worked effectively in cross-functional teams?
   - Do they demonstrate empathy and respect for others?
   - Scoring: 1 (Poor) to 5 (Excellent)

4. Adaptability & Resilience
   - How do they handle change and ambiguity?
   - Can they recover gracefully from failures?
   - Scoring: 1 (Poor) to 5 (Excellent)

5. Leadership & Initiative
   - Have they taken ownership of projects or problems?
   - Do they inspire and motivate others?
   - Scoring: 1 (Poor) to 5 (Excellent)

6. Work Ethic & Accountability
   - Are they self-driven and results-oriented?
   - Do they own their mistakes and learn from them?
   - Scoring: 1 (Poor) to 5 (Excellent)

SECTION 3: STANDARD INTERVIEW QUESTIONS
-----------------------------------------
OPENING QUESTIONS:
Q1. Tell me about yourself and your professional background.
    - Look for: clear narrative, relevant highlights, confident delivery
    - Red flags: rambling, no structure, irrelevant personal details

Q2. Why are you interested in this role/company?
    - Look for: company research, genuine motivation, role alignment
    - Red flags: generic answers, focusing only on salary/benefits

BEHAVIORAL QUESTIONS:
Q3. Tell me about a time you faced a significant challenge at work. How did you handle it?
    - Look for: STAR format, problem ownership, constructive outcome
    - Red flags: blaming others, no resolution, vague answer

Q4. Describe a situation where you had to work with a difficult colleague. What did you do?
    - Look for: empathy, communication, professional conflict resolution
    - Red flags: badmouthing, avoiding the conflict, no resolution

Q5. Give an example of a time you missed a deadline. What happened and what did you learn?
    - Look for: accountability, corrective action, lessons learned
    - Red flags: denying any failure, excessive blame, no learning

Q6. Tell me about a time you led a project or initiative. What was the outcome?
    - Look for: scope, leadership style, measurable results
    - Red flags: no concrete outcome, passive role, team credit only

Q7. Describe a situation where you had to adapt to a major change at work.
    - Look for: flexibility, positive attitude, practical steps taken
    - Red flags: resistance to change, negativity, no adaptation shown

SITUATIONAL QUESTIONS:
Q8. If you were assigned a project with an unclear scope and tight deadline, what would you do?
    - Look for: clarification-seeking, prioritization, proactive approach
    - Red flags: paralysis, guessing without verification, overwhelmed response

Q9. How would you handle receiving critical feedback from your manager?
    - Look for: receptiveness, growth mindset, action steps
    - Red flags: defensiveness, dismissal, emotional reaction

Q10. If two team members had a conflict that was affecting productivity, how would you step in?
     - Look for: mediation skills, fairness, outcome-focus
     - Red flags: taking sides, ignoring it, escalating without reason

CLOSING QUESTIONS:
Q11. Where do you see yourself in 3–5 years?
     - Look for: ambition aligned with role, realistic planning, company fit
     - Red flags: vague, unrealistic, or completely misaligned goals

Q12. Do you have any questions for us?
     - Look for: thoughtful, role-specific questions about team, culture, growth
     - Red flags: only asking about salary, no questions at all

SECTION 4: EVALUATION RUBRIC
------------------------------
SCORE DEFINITIONS:
5 - Exceptional: Answer exceeds expectations, highly specific, excellent structure
4 - Strong: Clear, well-structured, meets all expected criteria
3 - Adequate: Satisfactory answer but lacks depth or specificity
2 - Weak: Vague, missing key elements, minimal insight
1 - Poor: Does not answer the question, irrelevant, or alarming red flags

OVERALL HIRING RECOMMENDATION:
- 45–60 points: Strong Hire
- 30–44 points: Hire with reservations
- 15–29 points: Revisit / Hold
- Below 15:    Do Not Hire

SECTION 5: DIVERSITY, EQUITY & INCLUSION GUIDELINES
-----------------------------------------------------
- Avoid questions related to age, marital status, religion, nationality, pregnancy, or disability.
- Ensure all candidates are evaluated on the same rubric without bias.
- Use structured interviews to minimize unconscious bias.
- If unsure whether a question is appropriate, do not ask it.

SECTION 6: LEGAL COMPLIANCE REMINDERS
---------------------------------------
- Do NOT ask: "Do you have children or plan to?" (discriminatory)
- Do NOT ask: "What is your religious affiliation?" (discriminatory)
- Do NOT ask: "How old are you?" (discriminatory)
- Do NOT ask: "Have you ever filed a workers' compensation claim?" (discriminatory)
- Always document the interview and retain records per company policy.

SECTION 7: POST-INTERVIEW PROCESS
------------------------------------
1. Complete the scoring rubric within 24 hours of the interview.
2. Avoid discussing candidates with non-panel members before consensus.
3. Submit recommendations via the HR portal.
4. Provide written feedback to candidates who request it.
5. Respect candidate confidentiality at all times.
